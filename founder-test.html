Private test rules

Use sanitised text only.

1

Remove names

Replace real people, clients, suppliers, staff, and companies with simple labels such as Client A, Supplier B, or Staff Member C.

2

Remove details

Do not include email addresses, phone numbers, postal addresses, account numbers, passwords, private IDs, or confidential references.

3

Remove sensitive material

Do not paste legal, medical, financial, employment, disciplinary, personal, or client-identifying information.

4

Keep control

The test is about whether the signals are useful. Silent Sentinel surfaces possible issues. The human decides what matters.

What to send for testing

A small, cleaned-up sample is enough.

A useful test sample might be ten to twenty copied inbox snippets, message summaries, or thread extracts after names and sensitive details have been removed.

  • Overdue replies
  • Follow-up requests
  • Client questions
  • Supplier issues
  • Internal updates
  • Small warnings buried in ordinary messages

What not to send

No raw inbox dumps.

Silent Sentinel is not yet a secure inbox-processing product. The first proof test should not receive raw mailbox exports, full confidential threads, private documents, or sensitive personal information.

The honest first test is simple: use anonymised text and check whether the top five signals are genuinely useful.
